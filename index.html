Optimizing Video Streaming Quality at Low Bandwidth

with Static Resolution Maintenance

Revolutionary research combining H.266/VVC codec technology with intelligent quality optimization to maintain high-definition video streaming even under bandwidth constraints.

Read Abstract Download Papers

Bachelor of Information and Communication Technology Honours Degree

University of Sri Jayewardenepura • Faculty of Technology • 2024

Abstract

In the modern digital world, video streaming has become one of the key methods that transmit various information with a focus on high-definition (HD) and ultra-high-definition (UHD) quality. This research explores a pressing challenge involved with streaming videos, keeping up high-quality visuals anytime the network conditions change without changing the resolution settings.

An innovative algorithm is put forth that allows for maintaining static video resolution while dynamically optimizing other video characteristics like reducing noise, bit depth, and frame rate so that they can respond well enough when there are limitations on the bandwidth. The research harnesses H.266 (Versatile Video Coding or VVC) codec's advanced compression capabilities which offer superior compression efficiency than previous standards.

Research Overview

Understanding the challenges and innovations in modern video streaming technology

Research Problem

Traditional Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR) methods require resolution changes during low bandwidth, causing significant quality degradation and user experience issues.

Innovation

Our approach maintains static video resolution while dynamically optimizing noise reduction, bit depth, and frame rate using advanced H.266/VVC codec technology.

Impact

Addresses critical gaps in low-bandwidth streaming, providing consistent quality without resolution switching, improving user experience significantly.

Research Objectives

Main Objective

To develop an algorithm that maintains constant video quality over time despite different bandwidth conditions, as a good substitute for Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR), by incorporating H.266 codec and considering noise reduction, bit depth adjustment, and frame rate optimization rather than changing the video resolution.

Algorithm Design

Design an algorithm that utilizes the H.266 codec to manage bandwidth constraints without reducing video resolution.

Performance Testing

Test the algorithm's effectiveness in maintaining high video quality under low bandwidth conditions through comprehensive evaluation.

Comparative Analysis

Compare the proposed algorithm against existing ABR methods in terms of video quality preservation and user experience.
